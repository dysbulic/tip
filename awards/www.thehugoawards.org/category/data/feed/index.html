]]> http://www.thehugoawards.org/2019/06/hugo-history-updates-posted/feed/ 1 3473 1,848 Ballots Cast in 2013 Hugo Awards http://www.thehugoawards.org/2013/08/2013-hugo-ballot-count/ Tue, 20 Aug 2013 21:53:34 +0000 http://www.thehugoawards.org/?p=1836 Continue reading →]]> LoneStarCon 3 has announced that 1,848 valid final ballots were received for this year’s awards, representing a voter turnout (percentage of eligible members who voted) of 42%. The figure of 1,848 is the third-highest ballot count in history after Renovation, the 2011 Worldcon, and Chicon 7, the 2012 Worldcon. LoneStarCon 3 previously announced an all-time record of 1,343 nominating ballots, highlighting the ever-increasing interest in and support for the Hugo Awards.

The Hugo Awards will be announced at a ceremony at LoneStarCon 3 in San Antonio, Texas on Sunday, September 1, beginning at 8 PM Central Daylight Time.

]]> 1836 A Little Data http://www.thehugoawards.org/2010/04/a-little-data/ http://www.thehugoawards.org/2010/04/a-little-data/#comments Mon, 12 Apr 2010 15:17:03 +0000 http://www.thehugoawards.org/?p=922 Continue reading →]]> As part of the nominee announcement event, Aussiecon 4’s Hugo Award Administrator, Vincent Docherty, presented some slides with voting data. The following two are quite interesting.

This chart shows the number of nominating ballots received in recent years. Aussiecon 4 has done very well to set a new record for participation and significantly exceeding the performance of previous Australian Worldcons (marked A1 – A3).

This chart shows where voters came from, sorted by postal address. As usual, the majority of voters came from the US (there are a lot of science fiction fans in America). Canada and Australia / New Zealand also produced significant numbers. In all ballots were received from fans in twenty different countries.

Those of you wanting detailed data on the nominating ballots, including the lists of runners up, will have to wait. These numbers are never revealed until after the winners have been announced at Worldcon.
