Normal Distribution Table

The Standard Normal distribution table is used to look up the area under a normal curve from 0 to the given z-value. The rows represent the ones and tenths digits while the columns are the hundredths digits. So, finding the area between 0 and 1.96 would take the row 1.9 and the column 0.06. The value is 0.475. This means that to the area to the left of that point is 0.475 + 0.5 = 0.975, (since the entire area is 1 and the curve is symmetric about 0). The area to the right then is 1 - 0.5 - 0.475 = 0.025.
