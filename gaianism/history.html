The Rationale for a Church

I want to live in a theocracy. I want all the land to be held by a Church. An atheistic Church growing out of the Christian tradition, but positing that the Holy Spirit is a cognitive mode rather than the manifestation of an external deity.

One explanation is that different modes inhabit the body as separate regions of the brain and different configurations of persistent muscular tension.

It is an attempt to fill what is seen as much needed social organization to accompany an anarchist political system.

My priorities are the ends of war, homelessness, and hunger. My plan is to offer a public alternative to Amazon. Everyone gets a wage, and everything beyond that goes into various funding pools â€“ proportions to be decided by the congregation.

It helps to know that another person is familiar with your basic moral tenants and, if not agrees, at least respects them. That's only really possible on a large scale if the tenants are simple and enunciated.

Thus, ten commandments.

I'm not attached to many of those, but I think it needs to be a set of either five, ten, or thirteen. Something, anything, so long as an interested party will likely not find it objectionable.

There also needs to be a credo. Something a believer could memorize and say as a group as opening or closing a service. "I believe that I have a pentapartite mind. I am constituted of selves housed in disparate parts of my brain and body. I believe in the Spirit. I believe I can allow that self to guide me. I believe all people can."

A greeting ritual might be to touch in some way and each side take at least one silent breath.

We need an alternative to the police. Someone to call when you might need restraintive force or want a cameraperson.

Rebel "Police" don't have a single jail where they take people. They find a permissible place where the person can be left and take them there.

Depending on the person's condition and reason for being ejected from the last location and history, they might have to be taken to the woods or something.

I want for property to be respected, but land is not property, it outlives an owner. I want for the Church to control it and everyone to be a part of the church.

I want something with a clear set of guidelines for appropriate behavior and extreme permissiveness for anything not conscripted.

A set of straightforward principles to build communities on. An ethical organization first and a commercial entity second.
