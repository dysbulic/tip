Okay, at the risk of being severely flamed (and I feel I need to say that this isn't directed at anyone in particular) I have to say that, for me, "because the Bible says so" isn't a good enough reason to qualify something as right or wrong. Also, just because it's in the Bible doesn't mean it actually happened. The reason I say this is, for those of you who don't know or choose to ignore it, the Bible contains many contradictions. Have you ever noticed that there are two creation stories? (There are in the Catholic Bible, anyway) Or how about the fact that Abraham had a son by a concubine, and, Isaac (I think) had two wives. These can be used to support polygamy, since in the context it was okay. I guess the point I'm trying to make is that fact should not be sought after in the Bible, just truth. The difference between truth and fact is that truth is universal and can be applied to a situation independent of place, time, and circumstances.

With this in mind, we can start to recognize truth in other religious works, such as the Koran (Islam, for those of you who didn't read), the Upanishads (Hinduism), and the Tao Te Ching (Taoist), etc. Of course, as a Catholic, I believe the Bible has greater importance than all others, but I can't just ignore the beliefs of over half the world's population just because I don't agree with every little bit of doctrine. Over the centuries every culture has been able to decipher at least a little bit of what is "good" and "moral," so, if given the opportunity, we should look to see if we can learn from them, if even just a little bit.
