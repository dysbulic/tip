Doomfire Prehistory - introduction to the new age

Doomfire was a strong clan that roamed with the reckless abandon of those who know that there is no force that they fear to face. The mention of the clan named "Doomfire" struck terror into the hearts of the elven and human kinds. And indeed, it was no force of the fair folk that eventually ended Doomfire's long, proud tradition of domination in war. It was a plague.

Much to the delight of humans and elves, a plague swept the region that killed off a majority of the Orks. This plague came to be known simply as the Ork Plague. Only the long-standing alliance with Mahirim kept the humans and elves from wiping out the Orks altogether. This Ork Plague claimed the life of Urk the Smasher, Warchief of Doomfire, and the clan did mourn for a long time. Doomfire Clan had been dealt a strong blow, but in the end it was their hunger to reclaim their previous glory that drove them to move on.

It was Urk's dying wish that Morlem be declared Warchief and seven days after Urk's death he faced the trials of the swamp and sought guidance from Azhi Dahaka. Morlem returned at dawn of the tenth day after he set out, and with him there was another Ork, a stranger to the clan. His brethren could see a new fire in Morlem's eyes that made them sure their days as a wounded race would not be long.

They soon learned that the Ork that traveled with Morlem was Irek, and that his clan had made their home deeper in the swamp and had been hit harder by the plague. Irek told a horrowing tale of watching his entire clan die to the plague, and wondering at why he never even got a little sick. He had heard of no other Orks who were completely immune to the plague and knew it was a sign from Azhi Dahaka that his purpose had not yet been fulfilled, but he had no idea what that purpose might be until he and Morlem had met on a desolate marsh plain deep in the heart of Morak.

Morlem declared that very night that he would form a council of three to rule over the Clan. Morlem was, of course, the first of the three, and Irek was to be the second. Normally the Orks would have challenged Irek as an outsider to the clan, but they could see that any such challenge would have been foolhardy as well as suicidal. Azhi Dahaka had clearly granted Irek a strength and vision that was not to be taken lightly. There was oddly enough no third declared. Most of the Orks correctly understood this to mean that someone would have to prove themselves worthy before they could be on the council.

And thus... a new age begins.
