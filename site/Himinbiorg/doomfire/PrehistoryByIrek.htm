Doomfire Prehistory - Telling by Irek

Dark was the day I left the former borders of my old clan's territory. I had wandered among the corpses of my family and friends for days before finally coming to the realization that it was time to move on. Why the plague never affected me, still I dont know. I could only drop to my knees and thank Azhi Dahaka that I too was not consumed by the furious evil that took all who I knew and called friend.

I wandered into the swamps, living off the raw meat of the swamp animals who dared to cross my path. It is said that when an ork reverts to his true roots and turns back to raw meat, he himself becomes almost like the wild animal of his recent past. I can say from experience that this was true for me. I barely remember those days, as they seemed to wash over me without much thought or notice of the passage of time. I remember killing two orks, and even a mahirim with my bare hands, though tbe last left terrible wounds. I was hungry...and a creature consumed with darkness.

My next clear memory is one day approaching a small clearing in the swamp. I saw an ork fighting a gigantic swamp beast, the kind of which I had tried my best to avoid all my life. I thought for sure at any moment the beast would end the ork's life with one giant swing of its claw, and yet every time I thought the ork's doom was at hand, I was surprised at his agility, strength and pure courage. As I watched this warrior fight, I slowly felt myself regaining my senses and remembering the honor that had once filled me as an ork warrior.

After what seemed like hours the beast finally fell. The ork himself, exhausted and injured from battle, immediately collapsed into the swampgrass. I made my way carefully towards him, and was not surprised when, upon hearing my approach, he leapt up and brandished his weapon at me. He then noticed the scars and damaged armor which covered my body, lowered his weapon and spoke to me. I, feeling once again as though Azhi Dahaka had placed me on a path of divine providence, offered my hand in friendship, instead of battle, to this brave warrior.

After a brief talk, Morlem and I ate of the body of the swamp beast, and thus declared ourselves friends. In the next few days that followed, we became blood brothers also, and upon return to the Doomfire encampment, I became friends with the rest of the survivors of the clan and was welcomed back into warrior ranks of my ork brothers....
